Recruiter Screening and Explanation Rules

Purpose
This document helps an assistant produce better short_explanation outputs for ranking results. It is useful for recruiter-facing and candidate-facing scenarios because it clarifies what signals matter most during matching.

Primary Screening Dimensions
Skill overlap:
How many required skills are present and how strong the evidence is.

Experience alignment:
Whether years of experience and scope of work fit the vacancy level.

Seniority alignment:
Whether the candidate shows the expected ownership and complexity for Junior, Middle, or Senior roles.

Location and work mode:
Whether the candidate location and preferred locations align with on-site, hybrid, or remote expectations.

Domain fit:
Whether the candidate has worked in comparable business or product contexts.

Evidence strength:
Whether the resume shows measurable impact, not only a list of tools.

How to Write a Strong Explanation
A good explanation should mention:
the strongest matched skills
one or two fit signals beyond raw skills
the most important missing skill or gap, if relevant
the likely reason the candidate or vacancy ranks above alternatives

Examples of Good Explanation Logic
"Strong fit because the candidate matches FastAPI, PostgreSQL, Docker, and backend API ownership, with experience slightly above the vacancy minimum."

"Moderate fit because the candidate has React and TypeScript experience, but lacks testing depth and clear evidence of scalable frontend architecture."

"Promising fit for an entry-level data role because SQL, Excel, and dashboarding are present, though statistical experimentation is still limited."

Screening Red Flags
Many tools but no outcomes.
Seniority title not supported by scope.
Missing core requirements masked by optional skills.
Unclear role target.
No evidence for the most important claimed skills.

How the Assistant Should Prioritize
Do prioritize required_skills over optional_skills.
Do treat measurable impact as a ranking advantage.
Do use seniority and experience as meaningful constraints.
Do mention location mismatch when it affects feasibility.
Do keep explanations concise but evidence-based.
