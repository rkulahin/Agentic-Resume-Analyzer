Career Growth Framework Across Technical and Business Roles

Purpose
This document helps an assistant produce growth-oriented advice after matching or gap analysis. It is useful when the user asks what to do next to reach a higher seniority level or change domains.

Growth Signals by Stage
Junior:
Learns quickly, delivers scoped tasks, responds to feedback, builds reliable habits, and develops one clear domain strength.

Middle:
Owns features or workstreams end-to-end, makes sound execution decisions, communicates trade-offs, and improves team reliability.

Senior:
Owns systems or functions, influences priorities, mentors others, makes strategic trade-offs, and improves processes beyond individual delivery.

How Growth Looks in Different Domains
Engineering:
Junior to mid growth means stronger debugging, testing, architecture awareness, and delivery ownership.
Mid to senior growth means system thinking, reliability, mentoring, and business-context decision making.

Data:
Junior to mid growth means stronger SQL, clearer metrics, and better stakeholder communication.
Mid to senior growth means KPI ownership, experimentation strategy, and cross-functional influence.

QA:
Junior to mid growth means stronger strategy, automation depth, and risk assessment.
Mid to senior growth means quality leadership, release confidence, and process design.

Marketing:
Junior to mid growth means owning channels and reporting.
Mid to senior growth means growth strategy, budget trade-offs, and business impact leadership.

Audit and Tax:
Junior to mid growth means more independent analysis, documentation quality, and issue identification.
Mid to senior growth means advisory confidence, risk prioritization, and stakeholder influence.

Growth Recommendations the Assistant Can Give
Improve depth in one primary stack or domain.
Add one adjacent skill that expands role eligibility.
Collect stronger evidence of impact.
Take ownership of a process, not only tasks.
Document achievements in measurable terms.
Practice communication with non-specialist stakeholders.

What Not to Recommend
Do not recommend collecting random certifications without role relevance.
Do not recommend broadening too early without a clear target.
Do not frame growth only as "learn more tools."
Do not ignore business communication and ownership when discussing seniority.
