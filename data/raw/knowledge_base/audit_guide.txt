Audit Guide: Matching Criteria, Signals, and Advice

Purpose
This guide supports retrieval for Audit Specialist, Internal Auditor, and Audit Associate roles.

Core Skills Recruiters Expect
Audit fundamentals:
audit procedures
control testing
risk assessment
documentation quality
sampling logic
finding analysis

Financial and reporting literacy:
financial statements
financial reporting
IFRS or GAAP awareness
materiality concepts
process understanding

Operational and compliance perspective:
internal controls
policy review
compliance checks
process walkthroughs
stakeholder interviews

Tools and analysis support:
Excel
ERP or SAP familiarity
evidence organization
basic data analysis

Signals of a Strong Candidate
Can explain how to assess risk and test controls.
Documents findings clearly and professionally.
Understands how business processes connect to financial and compliance risk.
Can identify control weaknesses and recommend practical improvements.
Communicates well with process owners and management.

Important Resume Evidence
Types of audits performed.
Industries or processes reviewed.
Control testing and reporting responsibilities.
Documentation rigor.
Improvements or risk reductions influenced by the candidate.

Common Gaps
Finance background without audit procedure depth.
Audit support work without risk or control reasoning.
No reporting standards awareness.
Heavy tool focus but weak judgment and documentation.
No stakeholder-facing experience.

Advice by Seniority
Junior:
Show audit support tasks, documentation quality, Excel proficiency, and understanding of controls and evidence.

Middle:
Show ownership of sections of the audit cycle, stronger risk reasoning, and communication of findings.

Senior:
Show audit planning, stakeholder management, issue prioritization, and recommendations that influence controls or governance.

What to Emphasize in Matching Explanations
Matched_skills should prioritize audit procedures, controls, risk assessment, reporting literacy, and documentation quality.
Missing_skills should prioritize control-testing depth and standards awareness before secondary tooling.
Strong explanations should mention judgment, evidence quality, and business risk understanding.
